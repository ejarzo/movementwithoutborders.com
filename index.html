Judson Memorial Church presents

Movement Without Borders

A Day of Performance To Benefit New Sanctuary Coalition

September 26, 2020 11AM — 6PM
Judson Memorial Church
55 Washington Square South, New York, NY

The event is free and open to the public!
Suggested admission $20 at door

“Because of the transient nature of human life, migration is the starting point for everybody.”
Moshin Hamid

On Saturday, September 26th, 2020, just before the critical national elections, dancers, musicians, filmmakers, and poets will gather in community around the urgent issue of humanizing our immigration system. Please join us for a day of performance that surpasses boundary. One that will celebrate immigration as integral to the DNA of America and has humanity at its core.

Conceived, directed and produced by dance maker Richard Colton, in collaboration with Judson Memorial Church, the event aims to benefit New Sanctuary Coalition, a multi-faith immigrant-led organization, founded in 2007, that creates support systems for those navigating the immigration system.

Dance

Performers will include dancer/choreographers Catherine Ellis Kirk, Edivaldo Ernesto, Ernesto Breton, Francisco Cordova, Horacio Macuacua, Jimena Paz, Kayla Farrish, Marcella Lewis, and Mariana Valencia,

Music

Special Guest, drummer/composer Antonio Sanchez & Migration with singer Thana Alexa and poet Jonathan Mendoza performing from Sanchez's 2019 album Lines in the Sand, the brilliant music protest against injustice in our current political climate and a tribute to every immigrant’s journey.

Film

The event will include short documentary films from the Humano International Film Festival, Adelita Husni-Bey’s Chiron and the border photojournalism of Tish Lampert, presented by the artist.

Speakers

Poet Claudia Rankine and The Racial Imaginary Institute with visual artist Xaviera Simmons, Hermes Lopez, Director of Humano Film Festival @ The Tijuana Cultural Center, Enrique Morones, Founder/Director of Border Angels, Michele Lampach, Founder and Executive Director of UnLocal, Ravi Ragbir, Executive Director, and Janice Hoseine, Program Director of New Sanctuary Coalition; members of New York Immigration Coalition, Roger H. Brown, President of Berklee College of Music, and Reverend Micah Bucey of Judson Memorial Church.
