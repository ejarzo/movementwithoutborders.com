Judson Memorial Church presents

Movement Without Borders

A Day of Performance to Benefit the New Sanctuary Coalition NYC

September 26, 2020 11AM — 6PM
Judson Memorial Church, New York
The event is free and open to the public with a suggested donation of $15/$25 (TBD) to benefit New Sanctuary Coalition NYC

“Because of the transient nature of human life, migration is the starting point for everybody.” (MOSHIN HAMID)

New York, NY — On Saturday, September 26th, 2020, just before the critical national elections, dancers, musicians, filmmakers, and poets will gather in community around the urgent issue of humanizing our immigration system.

Movement Without Borders: A Day of Performance to Benefit New Sanctuary Coalition NYC will take place from 11AM to 6PM at Judson Memorial Church, 55 Washington Square, New York, NY. It is a day of performance and arts events with a reach that surpasses boundary.

Conceived, directed and produced by dance maker Richard Colton, in collaboration with Judson Memorial Church, the event aims to benefit New Sanctuary Coalition NYC, a multi-faith immigrant-led organization, founded in 2007, that creates support systems for those navigating the immigration system.

Performers

Performers will include dancer/choreographers Mariana Valencia, Jimena Paz, Ernesto Breton, Francisco Cordova, Horacio Macuacua, Edivaldo Ernesto, and composer/drummer Antonio Sanchez & Migration

Speakers

Among the Speakers will be poet Claudia Rankine and The Racial Imaginary Institute; Xaviera Simmons, visual artist; Hermes Lopez, Director of Humano Film Festival @ Tijuana Cultural Center; Enrique Morones, Founder/Director of Border Angels; Michele Lampach, Founder and Executive Director of UnLocal; Ravi Ragbir, Executive Director, and Janice Hoseine, Program Director of New Sanctuary Coalition NYC; members of New York Immigration Coalition, Roger H. Brown, President of Berklee College of Music, and Reverend Micah Bucey of Judson Memorial Church.

Film

The event will also include short documentary films from the Humano International Film Festival, Adelita Husni-Bey’s Chiron and the border photojournalism of Tish Lampert, presented by the artist.

Movement Without Borders has received major support from The Doris Duke Charitable Foundation.
