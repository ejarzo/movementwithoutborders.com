Judson Memorial Church presents

Movement Without Borders

A Day of Performance Around Issues of Migration To Benefit New Sanctuary Coalition

September 26, 2020 11AM — 6PM
Judson Memorial Church
55 Washington Square South, New York, NY

The event is free and open to the public!
Donations will be accepted at the door - proceeds benefit New Sanctuary Coalition

“Because of the transient nature of human life, migration is the starting point for everybody.”
Moshin Hamid

On Saturday, September 26th, 2020, just before the critical national elections, dancers, musicians, filmmakers, and poets will gather in community around the urgent issue of humanizing our immigration system. It is a day of performance and arts events with a reach that surpasses boundary.

Conceived, directed and produced by dance maker Richard Colton, in collaboration with Judson Memorial Church, the event aims to benefit New Sanctuary Coalition, a multi-faith immigrant-led organization, founded in 2007, that creates support systems for those navigating the immigration system.

Dance

Performers will include dancer/choreographers Mariana Valencia, Jimena Paz, Ernesto Breton, Francisco Cordova, Horacio Macuacua and Edivaldo Ernesto

Music

Special Guest, drummer/composer Antonio Sanchez & Migration with singer Thana Alexa and poet Jonathan Mendoza performing from Sanchez's 2019 album Lines in the Sand, the brilliant music protest against injustice in our current political climate and a tribute to every immigrant’s journey.

Film

The event will also include short documentary films from the Humano International Film Festival, Adelita Husni-Bey’s Chiron and the border photojournalism of Tish Lampert, presented by the artist.

Speakers

Among the Speakers will be poet Claudia Rankine and The Racial Imaginary Institute; Xaviera Simmons, visual artist; Hermes Lopez, Director of Humano Film Festival @ Tijuana Cultural Center; Enrique Morones, Founder/Director of Border Angels; Michele Lampach, Founder and Executive Director of UnLocal; Ravi Ragbir, Executive Director, and Janice Hoseine, Program Director of New Sanctuary Coalition; members of New York Immigration Coalition, Roger H. Brown, President of Berklee College of Music, and Reverend Micah Bucey of Judson Memorial Church.
